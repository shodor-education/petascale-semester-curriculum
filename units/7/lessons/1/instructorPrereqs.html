Before teaching this lesson, the instructor should have:

  • Good C programming experience.
  • A firm understanding of language syntax and C-style programming (pass-by-value, pointers, structs, preprocessor, functions).
  • A firm understanding of memory allocation.
  • A good understanding of CPU architecture (ALU, registers, memory hierarchy, caching, SIMD execution units).
  • An understanding of threads (pthreads at the least).
  • Exposure to the idea of SIMD (Single-Instruction, Multiple Data) programming concept.
  • Exposure to the idea of SPMD (Single-Instruction, Multiple Program) programming concept.
