By the end of this lesson, students will:

  • Knowledge of what is heterogenous memory/computing with OpenACC and its use cases.
  • How to find the compute intensive parts of programs.
  • Understand the two methods for adding compiler directives:
    • One where the program flow and data flow is controlled by the programmer.
    • One where all the work is left for the compiler to decide which loops to parallelize and how to copy data between CPU <=> GPU.
  • Learn by example how to avoid bad practices in OpenACC programming that could lead to potential performance issues.
  • Hands-on examples and exercises for accelerating scientific applications using PGI compiler.
