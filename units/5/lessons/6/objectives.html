By the end of this module, students will:

  1. Have better understanding of how to measure speedup and efficiency of MPI applications
  2. Understanding of Amdahl’s Law for measuring speedup and scalability of MPI applications
  3. Able to differentiated between strong and weak scaling when measuring performance of MPI applications
  4. Have the ability to implement MPI’s time measuring routines such as MPI_Wtime() for finding the most time consuming part of code.
  5. Explore the implementation of different MPI modes either blocking vs. non-blocking or point-to-point vs collective communication modes for improving MPI application’s communications performance.
